Everyone is slapping AI onto things right now. Copilots. Assistants. Agents. Most of it is the same old software with a chat box bolted on the side.

When I say AI-native, I mean something different.

I mean building in a way where AI isn't a feature — it's the foundation. The workflow is designed around it from the start. The decisions, the tooling, the pace of iteration — all of it assumes AI is in the loop.

For me that looks like this: I have an idea, I open a conversation, and I start building. Not planning to build someday. Building now. The gap between thinking something and having a working version of it has collapsed in a way that changes everything about how I approach problems.

That's what AI-native means to me. Not AI as an add-on. AI as the default creative partner.

The implications for product leadership are significant. If you can prototype faster than your team can write a PRD, the whole process changes. Discovery looks different. Validation looks different. What counts as "ready to build" looks different.

I'm still figuring out what that means at scale. But I know it's real, and I know most teams aren't there yet.
